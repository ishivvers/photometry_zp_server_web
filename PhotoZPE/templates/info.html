Photometric Estimates

This site was designed to produce accurate estimates of the photometric zeropoint between a set of observations (instrumental magnitudes) and the standard photometric systems of SDSS, USNOB1, and 2-MASS. To do this, I pull observations from the SDSS catalog (if within the SDSS footprint) or from the all-sky USNO-B1 catalog, cross-match them to sources in the all-sky 2-MASS catalog, and then fit a model spectra from the Pickles Stellar Atlas to each source. This produces an estimate for the spectrum for each individual source, from which I then produce synthetic photometry for all requested bands. These photometric estimates can then be used to calculate a systematic offset between observed magnitudes (e.g. the instrumental magnitudes generated through photometry on images) and the true magnitudes for a set of observations; i.e. the photometric zeropoint.

Characteristic Errors

Though the estimates for individual sources can, at worst, be off by as much as .5 mag, I have worked to get the mean offset for any ensemble of sources as close to zero as possible. This works much better for central bands (i, z, y) than the bluer bands. Within our group, we use this system to estimate y-band zeropoints, which has characteristic errors very similar to those of z listed below - please be aware of the errors and limitations if you use this for blue bands out of the SDSS footprint!
When using SDSS (e.g. to predict y-band within the SDSS footprint), characteristic errors of individual sources are of order .10 mag, and errors of the mean offset for all sources in field are of order .05 mag.
