Welcome to PhotoZPE, the All-Sky Photometric Zeropoint Estimation Engine.

This site uses a all-sky catalogs along with synthetic photometry to produce photometric observations or estimates of sources across the entire sky in u, g, r, i, z, y, B, V, R, I, J, H & K passbands. The main goal of this site is to provide a robust and simple way to estimate photometric zeropoints of an astronomical image for any band in any part of the sky.
Please read the info page for a complete description, a discussion of errors, and a few examples.

Other usages:

This site can also produce matched catalogs and zeropoints of uploaded star lists - see submission forms to the right. These both require space-separated or tab-separated ASCII files with no blank lines (use # to comment out lines), with all coordinates in decimal degrees. Designed for stars grouped together in a field and limited to fields of 1° (3600") or less.

There is also an API, for automated queries using a program like curl or wget. See here for more information.
